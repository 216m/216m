Introduction Track

The introduction to the mindfulness activities involves a short description of breathing and relaxing. The track asks “Why is relaxing good?” This can be an excellent starting question to discuss why the Rainforest mindfulness activities are important. What does it mean to relax? What does it mean to be grateful together?


Note that each track includes instructions for the child, then time after for the child to complete the activity. When the child is done, they are prompted to turn the rainstick to go to the next activity.

Activity 1

Stretch and breath. The first activity asks the child to stretch their arms and bring them to their side to help use movement while relaxing.  The activity also asks the child to be conscious of their breathing while doing so. The purpose of this activity is to get the child moving and have them start to become aware of their surroundings and themselves.


Your child should take around 70-90 seconds to complete this activity.

Activity 2

Awareness of breath. The second activity asks the child to sit down and breath. While taking deep breath, the child is asked to place one hand on their chest and the other on their stomach. Having the child feel their hands move up and down while breathing helps facilitate an active awareness of their breath. You can talk with your child about activities that involved sitting and whether they like it or not. Perhaps they can incorporate this breath awareness into their sitting activities in the future.


Your child should take around 90-110 seconds to complete this activity.

Activity 3

Candle flickering for breath control. The third activity uses the imagery of a candle to help the child continue to be aware of their breath. The child is asked to sit down and put their finger up to their lips to visualize a candle. While they breath in and out, they should think about a flame flickering back and forth, but never going out. The purpose of this activity is to help the child visualize their breath and give them a tangible analogy to help slow their breath. You can talk with your child about the speed of their breath, if they ever thought about it before, and if it is easy or hard for them.


Your child should take around 120-150 seconds to complete this activity.

Activity 4

Waking up the body. The last activity asks the child to stand up and stretch to wake up the body. The child is asked to reach for the sky, then down to the ground, and repeat. The purpose of this activity is to incorporate movement in the closing activity and allow the child to “wake up” their mind and body. You can discuss the activities with your child after they are completed and how their experience was.


Your child should take around 70-90 seconds to complete this activity.
